Our Story:

With the covid pandemic driving Filipinos out of the workforce, a lot of families have lost the ability to put food on the table. Coupled with government negligence, community pantries were set up across different regions in the Philippines, as a sign of camaraderie, patriotism, what the Filipino masses are capable of.

We at E-bigay have noticed a significant problem with the setup of a crude stand flocked by hundreds of people at a time amidst a pandemic. As such, we have started the initiative to digitalize the 'lining up' and claiming of ayuda, not aiming to fully replace the concept of a traditional community pantry, rather to lessen the load, and give people the peace of mind of not having to risk catching covid, just to have food put on their tables.
